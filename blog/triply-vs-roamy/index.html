Triply vs Roamy: Which App Is Better for Saving Travel Spots?

Apr 11, 2026 · 6 min read

Triply and Roamy actually solve a similar problem: you see a place in a Reel, and you want to save it before you forget. Both apps use AI to extract locations from short-form video. That alone puts them in a different league from most travel apps, which still expect you to type in place names manually.

But the similarity ends at extraction. Roamy builds outward from there into itineraries, route planning, and multi-platform support. Triply stays focused on doing the capture part well. Which approach is better depends on what you actually need.

What They Have in Common

Both apps let you share a travel video and get back a list of places, mapped and saved. Both are on iOS. Both recognize that social media is where travel discovery happens now, and both built their product around that insight. If you're coming from the world of manually Googling place names you half-heard in a TikTok, either app is a massive upgrade.

They also both offer map views of your saved places and let you organize spots into collections or lists. The core loop -- see video, share to app, get places -- works in both.

On paper, Roamy wins on feature count. That's not really the question though.

Where Roamy Wins

TikTok Support

If you discover travel on TikTok as much as Instagram, Roamy has a clear advantage. Triply only works with Instagram Reels right now. For people who split their scrolling across both platforms, Roamy covers more ground.

Itinerary Building and Route Optimization

Roamy doesn't stop at saving places. Once you've collected spots, you can build a trip itinerary and optimize your route so you're not bouncing back and forth across a city. That's a real feature -- the kind of thing you'd otherwise need a separate app like Wanderlog for. Having extraction and planning in one place is convenient.

Scale and Community

With 2M+ travelers and 95K Instagram followers, Roamy is a more established product. Larger user base means more feedback, faster iteration, and a stronger social proof signal when you're deciding whether to trust an app with your travel planning.

Where Triply Wins

Simplicity

Triply does one thing: extract places from Instagram Reels and organize them on a map. No itinerary builder, no route planner, no trip scheduling. That's a feature, not a gap. The app is fast to learn, fast to use, and doesn't bury the extraction workflow under layers of trip planning UI. You share a Reel, you get your places. Done.

When an app tries to be the extraction tool AND the itinerary planner AND the route optimizer, every feature competes for attention. Triply bets that doing extraction cleanly matters more than doing five things adequately.

Destination Guides

Triply offers curated destination guides -- editorial content about places worth visiting, organized by city and category. If you're in the "I want to go somewhere but don't know where" phase, these guides give you a starting point that goes beyond whatever the algorithm serves you. Roamy doesn't have this.

Free Tier

Triply's core features are free. Extract from Reels, save to collections, view on map -- no paywall. For casual travelers who save a few Reels a week, that matters.

The real difference: Roamy is building an all-in-one travel platform -- extract, plan, optimize, go. Triply is building the best possible capture tool for Instagram Reels. If you want everything in one app and use TikTok too, Roamy is the obvious pick. If you want a clean, focused way to turn Reels into a travel bucket list without the overhead, Triply is sharper at that specific job.

When Roamy Is the Better Choice

  • You discover travel content on TikTok, not just Instagram
  • You want to build itineraries inside the same app where you save places
  • Route optimization matters to you -- you're planning dense city trips
  • You prefer an established app with a large community

When Triply Is the Better Choice

  • Instagram Reels is your primary source of travel inspiration
  • You want a minimal, focused app -- not another platform to learn
  • You're collecting places over time, not planning a specific trip yet
  • You value destination guides and curated content alongside extraction
  • You want the core experience fully free

The Verdict

Roamy is the more complete product. More features, more platforms, bigger user base, and a pipeline that goes from video to itinerary. If you want one app to handle everything, it's a strong choice.

Triply is the more focused product. It doesn't try to replace your itinerary planner. It captures places from Instagram Reels with less friction and wraps that in destination content that helps you decide where to go. For people who just want their saved Reels turned into a real bucket list, it's the lighter, cleaner option.

Neither app is wrong. They reflect two different philosophies: do everything in one place, or do one thing without compromise.
