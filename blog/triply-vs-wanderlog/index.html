Triply vs Wanderlog: Which Travel App Actually Saves You Time?

Apr 11, 2026 · 7 min read

Wanderlog and Triply both show up when you search for travel planning apps. They both have maps. They both let you save places. But they solve fundamentally different problems, and if you pick the wrong one for what you actually need, you're going to be frustrated.

I've spent serious time with both, and here's the thing -- they're not really competitors. They're more like two different chapters of the same trip. But the differences matter, so let me break it down.

The Quick Comparison

See what I mean? These apps barely overlap. Wanderlog is an itinerary planner. Triply is a place-capture tool. The table tells that story pretty clearly, but the details make it even more obvious.

Where Wanderlog Wins

Itinerary Building Is Its Superpower

If you're the kind of person who plans trips day by day -- breakfast here, museum at 10am, lunch at that spot from the food blog, walk to the park in the afternoon -- Wanderlog is genuinely excellent at this. You can drag and drop activities, see them on a map, optimize your route so you're not zigzagging across a city. It's what a trip planner should feel like.

Triply doesn't do this at all. It's not trying to. There's no day-by-day view, no scheduling, no route optimization. If you need a detailed itinerary, Wanderlog is the clear winner.

Collaborative Planning

Planning a trip with friends? Wanderlog lets multiple people edit the same trip in real time. Add places, vote on restaurants, split the budget. It's like Google Docs for travel. This is a huge deal if you're coordinating a group trip and everyone has opinions (which, let's be honest, they always do).

Triply is a solo experience right now. You're building your own bucket list, your own collections. Great for personal discovery, not built for group coordination.

Budget Tracking

Wanderlog lets you track expenses, set budgets for different categories, and keep a running total of what your trip is costing. For backpackers and budget-conscious travelers, this is genuinely useful. You can see exactly where your money is going before you've even left.

Multi-Platform Availability

Wanderlog works on iOS, Android, and the web. You can plan on your laptop and check the itinerary on your phone while you're walking around Tokyo. Triply is iOS-only right now, which is a real limitation if you're on Android or prefer planning on a bigger screen.

Where Triply Wins

The Discovery-to-Save Pipeline

Here's where the fundamental difference shows up. Wanderlog assumes you already know where you want to go. You open the app, search for "best cafes in Lisbon," and add them to your trip. That works fine if you're the type to read travel blogs and guidebooks.

But most of us don't discover places that way anymore. We find them on Instagram. We see a reel of someone eating the most incredible-looking pasta in Rome and think "I need to go there." The problem is, the reel doesn't give you a Google Maps pin. The creator might mumble the name. It might flash on screen for a second and a half.

With Triply, you share that reel and the AI extracts every place mentioned -- the restaurant, the piazza it's near, the gelato shop the creator walked past. All of them, mapped and ready to save. With Wanderlog, you'd need to rewatch the reel, try to catch the names, search for each one manually, and add them one by one.

For one reel, that's mildly annoying. For the 50+ travel reels you save in a month? It's a dealbreaker.

Zero-Effort Place Capture

The entire point of Triply is that you don't have to do the work. You share a reel. The AI figures out the places. You tap to save them. That's it. There's no typing, no searching, no "wait, was that restaurant called Trattoria da Mario or Trattoria di Mario?"

Wanderlog requires manual input for everything. Every place, every address, every detail -- you're typing it in or searching for it. That's fine for a planned trip where you've done your research. It's terrible for the casual, ongoing process of "I saw something cool, I want to remember it."

Built for the Way You Actually Find Places

There's a gap between "I saw a cool place on Instagram" and "that place is in my trip plan." Wanderlog bridges the second half of that gap (place to itinerary). Triply bridges the first half (content to place). And honestly, the first half is the part where most people drop off. You see the reel, you save it in Instagram, and then it rots in your saved folder forever. Triply fixes that.

The Real Question: Discovery vs. Planning

This whole comparison boils down to one question: what stage of travel planning are you in?

  • Discovery stage: You're scrolling, finding cool places, building a wishlist. This is Triply territory. It's designed for passive, effortless capture of places you encounter in your feed.
  • Planning stage: You know where you're going. You need to build an itinerary, book hotels, coordinate with friends, figure out the budget. This is Wanderlog territory.

The mistake people make is using a planning tool for discovery (too much friction) or a discovery tool for planning (not enough features). They're different jobs.

How They Work Together

Here's what I actually do, and I think it's the best approach:

  1. Ongoing: Every time I see a travel reel I like, I share it to Triply. Takes two seconds. The places get extracted and saved into collections -- "Japan," "Portugal," "Someday Maybe."
  2. When I decide to take a trip: I open my Triply collection for that destination. I've got weeks or months of curated, reel-sourced places already there.
  3. Itinerary time: I pull my favorites from Triply into Wanderlog and build the actual day-by-day plan. Route optimization, scheduling, budget -- all of that happens in Wanderlog.

Triply is the funnel. Wanderlog is the organizer. Together, they cover the full journey from "that looks cool" to "we're going on Tuesday at 2pm."

My honest take: Wanderlog is a more full-featured app overall. It does more things. But Triply solves a problem that Wanderlog doesn't even acknowledge -- the gap between seeing a place on Instagram and having it in your travel plans. If you're someone who discovers places through Reels (and in 2026, who isn't?), you need something to bridge that gap. Triply does it better than anything else I've used.

When Wanderlog Is All You Need

Stick with just Wanderlog if:

  • You discover places through travel blogs, guidebooks, or Google searches rather than social media
  • You're planning a specific trip and need a full itinerary with scheduling
  • You're coordinating with travel partners and need real-time collaboration
  • You want budget tracking built into your planning tool
  • You're on Android

When You Need Triply

Triply makes more sense if:

  • You discover most of your travel ideas through Instagram Reels
  • You save dozens of travel reels but never actually extract the places
  • You want a passive, zero-effort way to build a travel bucket list over time
  • You're in the "dreaming and collecting" phase, not the "planning and booking" phase
  • You're tired of losing places because they're buried in your Instagram saved folder

The Verdict

Wanderlog is the better itinerary planner. There's no question about that. If you need to build a detailed, day-by-day schedule for your next trip, Wanderlog does it really well.

Triply is the better capture tool. If you need to turn Instagram Reels into saved, mapped, organized places without doing any manual work, Triply is the only app that does this properly.

They're not competitors. They're different stages of the same trip. And the smartest move is probably to use both -- Triply for the discovering and collecting, Wanderlog for the planning and scheduling. Your future traveling self will thank you for it.
