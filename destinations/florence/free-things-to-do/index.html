Free Things to Do in Florence

You don't need to spend a cent to have an incredible time in Florence. Here are the best free experiences the city has to offer.

No budget needed

Free activities in Florence

01

Visit the Duomo Exterior

The cathedral's exterior is covered in pink, white, and green marble that's staggering to see up close. Walk around the entire building — the details and the scale are best appreciated from the ground. Free.

02

Piazzale Michelangelo Sunset

The famous viewpoint over Florence is free and the sunset is legendary. The entire city spreads below with the Duomo, Palazzo Vecchio, and the Arno bridges perfectly framed.

03

Cross the Ponte Vecchio

Walking across Florence's medieval bridge lined with gold shops is free and iconic. Visit at dusk when the light is golden and the crowds thin slightly. Look for the Vasari Corridor windows above.

04

Free First Sundays at Museums

On the first Sunday of each month, state museums including the Uffizi, Accademia, and Pitti Palace are free. Queue early — the Uffizi line can be long but moves steadily.

05

Rose Garden Below Piazzale Michelangelo

A terraced garden with roses, sculptures, and views over the city. It's free, peaceful, and most tourists walk right past it on the way to Piazzale Michelangelo above.

06

San Lorenzo Market Browsing

The outdoor leather market around San Lorenzo church is free to wander. Haggling is expected and even if you don't buy, the energy of the market and the quality of the craftsmanship is impressive.

07

Walk the Oltrarno

Cross the river and explore the artisan neighbourhood on foot for free. Watch craftspeople at work through open workshop doors — gilders, mosaicists, and leather artisans keep centuries-old traditions alive.

08

Basilica di Santa Croce Piazza

The piazza in front of the famous church is free and grand — locals play football on the same stones where medieval jousts took place. The church facade is beautiful even if you skip the paid interior.
